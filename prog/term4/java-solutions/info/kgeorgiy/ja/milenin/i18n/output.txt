Blade
See also: Blade (character) § Film
By May 2013, Marvel Studios had a working script for a new Blade film after regaining the rights following New Line Cinema's prior film series.[103][104] In February 2019, Mahershala Ali approached Marvel Studios about starring in a new film after previously portraying Cornell "Cottonmouth" Stokes in Marvel Television's Luke Cage.[105] Kevin Feige officially announced the film with Ali in the title role at the July 2019 San Diego Comic-Con;[105][21] Ali first had an uncredited voice cameo as Blade in Eternals (2021).[106] In February 2021, Stacy Osei-Kuffour was hired to write the film.[94] Bassam Tariq had been hired to direct by September 2021.[93][107] Filming is expected to begin in July 2022,[108] at Trilith Studios in Atlanta, Georgia.[109][110] Blade will be released in a future MCU phase.[111]

Untitled Deadpool film
See also: Deadpool 2 § Future
After the acquisition of 21st Century Fox by Disney was announced in December 2017, Disney CEO Bob Iger said Ryan Reynolds would reprise his role as Wade Wilson / Deadpool from 20th Century Fox's R-rated X-Men films Deadpool (2016) and Deadpool 2 (2018) in the PG-13 rated MCU.[112][113] By December 2019, Reynolds confirmed a third Deadpool film was in development at Marvel Studios,[114] with Wendy Molyneux and Lizzie Molyneux-Logelin writing the film by November 2020,[115] when Reynolds' involvement and the film's R-rating were confirmed.[115][116] In January 2021, Feige confirmed the film's MCU setting.[116] In March 2022, Rhett Reese and Paul Wernick were hired to return from the first two films to rewrite the screenplay, while Shawn Levy was revealed to be directing.[95] Reynolds' production company Maximum Effort co-produces the film.[117] Filming is expected to begin after 2022.[118]
